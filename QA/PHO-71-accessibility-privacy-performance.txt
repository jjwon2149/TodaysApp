PHO-71 Accessibility, Privacy, and Performance QA

Accessibility

- Confirm the app follows the system appearance in both Light and Dark Mode. There is no app-level forced Light Mode policy.
- Test Dynamic Type at the largest accessibility size on Home, Calendar, Entry Editor, Entry Detail, Onboarding, and Profile. Primary rows should wrap or stack instead of overlapping.
- With VoiceOver enabled, confirm Home streak/progress/photo cards announce labels and values, not only decorative icon names.
- On Calendar, confirm the previous and next month buttons announce their direction and hint, and entry days announce whether a record exists.
- In Entry Editor, confirm the photo preview/empty state, camera button, library picker, memo field, mood choices, save button, and completion action have meaningful labels or hints.

Privacy

- Camera and photo library permission strings state that access is used only when the user captures or chooses a private daily entry photo.
- Camera permission is requested from the camera flow only; denied/restricted states show the Settings/Cancel prompt.
- Profile includes a privacy and storage section describing local storage, permission timing, and deletion behavior.

Performance

- Calendar cells use stored thumbnails when present and fall back to the original image only when needed.
- Local image rendering for Home, Calendar, and Entry Detail goes through LocalImageView, which decodes files off the main actor and caches decoded images.
- Launch maintenance still backfills missing thumbnails and deletes unreferenced entry media in the background.

Suggested Regression Pass

- Run unit tests and a simulator build before QA handoff.
- Populate at least 50 entries with thumbnails, scroll Calendar between several months, and verify no obvious main-thread hitching from image decode.
- Verify deleting an entry removes it from Calendar and does not leave the detail screen stuck while recalculating streaks.
